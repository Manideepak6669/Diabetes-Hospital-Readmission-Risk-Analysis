# 🏥 Diabetes Hospital Readmission Risk Analysis

## Transforming Healthcare Data into Actionable Insights

This project is a complete end-to-end Healthcare Data Analytics solution focused on identifying the key factors that contribute to hospital readmissions among diabetic patients.

Using real-world healthcare data, I designed and implemented a full analytics pipeline covering data cleaning, transformation, exploratory analysis, SQL querying, and interactive business intelligence reporting.

The objective was not simply to build dashboards, but to answer a critical healthcare business question:

**Why are diabetic patients being readmitted to hospitals, and how can healthcare providers reduce readmission risk while improving patient outcomes?**

---

## 🎯 Business Problem

Hospital readmissions are one of the biggest challenges in healthcare systems worldwide.

Frequent readmissions:

* Increase healthcare costs
* Impact hospital performance metrics
* Reduce operational efficiency
* Affect patient health outcomes

This project analyzes diabetic patient data to uncover the factors that drive readmissions and provide data-driven recommendations for healthcare decision-makers.

## 📈 Key Business Insights

### 1. Elderly Patients Represent the Highest Risk Group

Patients aged 70–90 demonstrated significantly higher readmission rates compared to younger populations.

### 2. Longer Hospital Stays Increase Readmission Risk

Patients staying more than 7 days were substantially more likely to return to the hospital after discharge.

### 3. Medication Burden Impacts Patient Outcomes

Patients prescribed a large number of medications showed a stronger tendency toward readmission.

### 4. Diagnosis Complexity Drives Readmission

Patients with multiple diagnoses and chronic complications exhibited higher readmission frequencies.

### 5. High-Risk Patient Profiles Can Be Identified Early

Combining age, hospital stay duration, medication count, and diagnosis complexity enables proactive identification of patients most likely to return.

---

## 📋 Recommendations

* Implement post-discharge follow-up programs for elderly patients.
* Establish medication counseling initiatives.
* Introduce high-risk patient monitoring systems.
* Develop preventive care strategies for complex cases.
* Use predictive risk scoring for discharge planning.
